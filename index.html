Digital Divide: NYC vs South Bend

Interactive visualizations comparing public Wi-Fi access, community internet resources, and digital infrastructure across two very different cities.

Interactive Plotly NYC Open Data South Bend Open Data
Overview NYC South Bend Sources

Project Overview

This project explores how access to digital infrastructure varies across cities, focusing on public Wi-Fi availability, computer access, and community-level indicators of digital equity.

Intended Audience

  • Students and residents seeking public internet access
  • Community advocates and policymakers
  • Anyone interested in digital inequality

Methods

  • Cleaned and standardized public datasets
  • Aggregated data by borough, neighborhood, and district
  • Created interactive Plotly visualizations

New York City

Public Wi-Fi access and digital infrastructure patterns

NYC Public Wi-Fi Hotspots — Locations

Insights

  1. Hotspots cluster in high-traffic areas.
  2. Coverage varies significantly by neighborhood.
  3. Public Wi-Fi helps reduce access barriers.

NYC Wi-Fi Hotspots by Neighborhood (NTA)

Insights

  1. Neighborhood-level disparities are visible.
  2. Some NTAs have far fewer hotspots.
  3. Useful for targeted infrastructure planning.

NYC Public Computer Centers — Locations

Insights

  1. Centers provide devices and stable access.
  2. Some areas rely heavily on these locations.
  3. Gaps suggest expansion opportunities.

Public Computer Centers by Borough

Insights

  1. Distribution varies widely by borough.
  2. Counts alone don’t reflect demand.
  3. Lower counts may indicate device deserts.

Wi-Fi Hotspots by Borough (Totals)

Insights

  1. Borough-level differences are substantial.
  2. Population density partially explains totals.
  3. Disparities remain after normalization.

Top 20 Neighborhoods (NTA) by Wi-Fi Hotspots

Insights

  1. Hotspots concentrate in a small set of NTAs.
  2. Tourism and commuting likely influence placement.
  3. Other areas may still have unmet need.
